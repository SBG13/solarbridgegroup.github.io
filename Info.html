Government Approved Community Solar

Community Solar is a government backed clean energy initiative that allows homeowners, renters, and small businesses to save money on electricity without installing solar panels.

Why Solar

SolarBridge Group connects customers to state-approved solar farms through the same community solar programs used by Think Energy and other national providers.

How It works

Once enrolled, you reserve a share of a local solar project that feeds power into the grid.

Benefits

As the farm generates power, you receive energy credits that reduce your monthly utility bill.

Backed by State Programs

Each state has its own Clean Energy Initiative or Community Solar Program authorized by public utility commissions (PUCs). These programs are:
Government-approved and regulated
Designed to help residents save without up-front costs
Structured to support clean energy goals at the state and federal level
Some examples: Maryland’s Community Solar Pilot Program
New York’s NY-Sun Program
Illinois’ Adjustable Block Program
Massachusetts SMART Program
And many others across over 20+ participating states

Why It’s Government Approved

These programs are supported by legislation like the Inflation Reduction Act and are regulated by your state’s Public Utility Commission or Department of Energy. 

Solar farms are built and maintained by licensed energy developers.
The program is certified and regulated through your state’s Public Service Commission or Department of Energy. You sign up through licensed solar providers like SolarBridge Group.
